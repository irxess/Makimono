Scones

Scones are flaky, savory and relatively quick to make. Often served with jam and clotted cream, one of my favorites for breakfast.

Ingredients

  • 280 g all-purpose flour
  • 16 g baking powder
  • 6 g salt
  • 50 g sugar
  • 90 g butter
  • 155 g whole milk
  • 1 egg

Directions

  • 280 g all-purpose flour
  • 16 g baking powder
  • 6 g salt
  • 50 g sugar
Mix dry ingredients: Mix together the flour, baking powder, salt and sugar in a bowl.
  • 90 g butter
Add butter: Add the butter with your fingers until the mixture has a sandy texture without chunks.
  • 155 g whole milk
  • 1 egg
Mix milk and egg: In a different bowl, mix the milk and egg together. If desired, save a bit of the egg for an egg wash later.
Mix everything: Add the milk mixture to the bowl with flour. Mix until it becomes a relatively smooth ball, but try to avoid kneading the dough too much.
Preheat oven: Preheat the oven to 200 degrees Celsius.
Shape: Roll the dough until it is about 1-2 cm thick. Cut out circles of the desired size and transfer them onto a baking sheet.
Bake: Put the scones into the oven, and bake at 200 degrees C with convection on. The scones should be done in about 14 minutes, until they have a nice, golden color on top.
